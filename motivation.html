Motivation


Theory

After doing some research on the topic 'Motivation' I came across
a few notable sources that helped me form my conclusion on what motivation
is. I believe that we get motivated once the pain of not doing something
becomes greater than the pain of actually doing it.

However, there is a big misconseption about motivation.
"Motivation is often the result of action, not the cause of it.
Getting started, even in very small ways, is a form of active inspiration
that naturally produces momentum" (Clear, 2012).

After doing some more digging I found out that there are
two main forms of motivation, internal and external.
Internal motivation according to Marinoff (2020) "[Is] when you do
something because it's internally fulfilling, interesting or
enjoyable-without an expectation of a reward or recognition from others."

On the other hand, external motivation is motivation
fueled from surrounding inputs in your life, like your family,
friends, school etc. These inputs can be both positive and negative.
If your boss would give you a raise this would be positive.
However, if your family tells you that you need to lose weight,
that would be a negative input. Although, this would still
motivate you to take action.
