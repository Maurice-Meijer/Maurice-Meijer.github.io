Motivation


Theory

After doing some research on the topic 'Motivation' I came across
a few notable sources that helped me form my conclusion on what motivation
is. I believe that we get motivated once the pain of not doing something
becomes greater than the pain of actually doing it.


However, there is a big misconseption about motivation.
"Motivation is often the result of action, not the cause of it.
Getting started, even in very small ways, is a form of active inspiration
that naturally produces momentum" (Clear, 2012).


After doing some more digging I found out that there are
two main forms of motivation, internal and external.
Internal motivation according to Marinoff (2020) "[Is] when you do
something because it's internally fulfilling, interesting or
enjoyable-without an expectation of a reward or recognition from others."


On the other hand, external motivation is motivation
fueled from surrounding inputs in your life, like your family,
friends, school etc. These inputs can be both positive and negative.
If your boss would give you a raise this would be positive.
However, if your family tells you that you need to lose weight,
that would be a negative input. Although, this would still
motivate you to take action.


After watching Dan Pink's Ted Talk I was surprised to
find out that motivation doesn't work the way you think it
does. He spoke about how external motivation works amazing
but takes away the creative process in people. He explained
this by using The Candle Problem. It showed that people who
were given a reward were slower at solving a creative problem
in oppose to the people who didn't recieve a reward.


This study caused a lot of confusion and reconsidiration.
Dan spoke about companies who got rid of their external motivation
and began to help employees with their internal motivation.
Dan spoke about a company that gives their employees 24 hours
to create whatever they want, as long as it has nothing to do
with their current tasks. This showed a greater productivity,
efficienty and happiness in the employees.



Performance Motivation

"Je bent in redelijke mate intrinsiek gemotiveerd en in redelijke mate
extrinsiek gemotiveerd. Dat je redelijk intrinsiek gemotiveerd bent
betekent dat je het best leuk vindt om te studeren. Maar heel leuk
vind je het nou ook weer niet. Dat je in redelijke mate extrinsiek
gemotiveerd bent wil zeggen dat je er ook rekening mee houdt wat je
met je studie kunt bereiken: een diploma, geld, status of de erkenning
van de mensen waar je van houdt. Op zich is er niks mis met jouw motivatie.
Wel zou je gelukkiger zijn als je studie wat beter zou passen bij je
werkelijke interesses." (123test.nl)


I agree with the results of 123test, I am motivated because I know
that I will need to study for a job but I wouldn't mind to study
less. It's not like I love to go to school.
